Tình Yêu Love
Stop I Young Love

Diễm Xưa Past Beauty

Huế, 1960

Mưa vẫn hay mưa trên hàng lá nhỏ
Buổi chiều ngồi ngóng những chuyến mưa qua...

"The rain still finds the small leaves to fall on / An afternoon sitting, watching the rains pass..."

Written for a young woman he watched from across a courtyard in Huế — he never spoke to her. She became the prototype for all his idealized loves: beautiful, unreachable, dissolving in rain. This song was his first sight of the strawberry.

Stop II Love in Wartime

Nối Vòng Tay Lớn Join Hands

Sài Gòn, 1968

Nối vòng tay lớn
Chúng ta đi từ đồng hoang vu qua bưng biền trống...

"Joining hands wide / We walk from barren fields through emptied marshlands..."

Written during the Tet Offensive when the war had reached every doorstep. TCS turned the romantic "join hands" into a call for an entire nation. Love could no longer be private — it had to be as wide as the country's suffering.

Stop III Love as Compassion

Biển Nhớ The Sea Remembers

Đà Lạt, 1962

Ngày mai em đi, biển nhớ tên em gọi về
Gọi hồn liễu rủ lê thê, gọi bờ xa đón bước về...

"Tomorrow you leave, the sea will miss your name and call it back / Calling the drooping willow-soul, calling the far shore to receive your return..."

By his early thirties, TCS's love songs had deepened. The beloved is no longer just a woman but a gathering place of all tenderness. The sea that remembers is love itself — patient, vast, holding everything that has passed through it.

Vô Thường Impermanence
Stop I Fleeting Youth

Như Cánh Vạc Bay Like a Stork Flying

Huế, 1961

Như cánh vạc bay, lặng lẽ vào đời
Như làn mây trôi, thấp thoáng mái đầu...

"Like a stork flying, quietly entering life / Like a drifting cloud, glimpsed above a rooftop..."

Among his earliest songs, written while still a student in Huế. Even then, he sensed that everything he loved was already in the process of leaving — not tragically, but naturally, like a bird that was never meant to land.

Stop II Impermanence in War

Đất Nước Motherland

Sài Gòn, 1967

Đất nước tôi thon thả giọt đàn bầu
Nghe buồn buồn vọng nước non xa...

"My country, slender as a monochord's drop / Hearing the sad echo from the distant land..."

As the American War intensified, TCS saw Vietnam as a body being unmade. Cities were destroyed, rebuilt, destroyed again. He experienced impermanence not as philosophy but as the daily fact of a country that refused to hold its shape.

Stop III Embracing Change

Một Cõi Đi Về A Realm of Coming and Going

Sài Gòn, 1980

Bao nhiêu năm rồi còn mãi ra đi
Đi đâu loanh quanh cho đời mỏi mệt...

"So many years and still always departing / Going in circles just to tire out life..."

Written in the post-reunification years, after TCS had lived long enough to trace his own circles. The song is not lament but recognition: coming and going are the same motion. Impermanence is not something that happens to us — it is us.

Cái Chết Death
Stop I Witnessing Death

Gia Tài Của Mẹ Mother's Legacy

Sài Gòn, 1965

Một ngàn năm đô hộ giặc Tầu
Một trăm năm đô hộ giặc Tây
Hai mươi năm nội chiến từng ngày...

"A thousand years under Chinese occupation / A hundred years under French occupation / Twenty years of civil war, every day..."

Banned by both sides during the war. TCS catalogued Vietnam's dead with the patience of a coroner — not to accuse, not to glorify, but to bear witness. The mother's legacy is a history of other people's dying. He made it impossible to look away.

Stop II Death as Companion

Hát Cho Một Người Nằm Xuống Song for Someone Lying Down

Sài Gòn, 1968

Anh nằm xuống sau một lần đã đến đây
Đến đây rồi anh sẽ không bao giờ đi nữa...

"You lie down after having come here once / Come here and you will never leave again..."

Written for a young soldier killed in 1968, the song refuses anger. TCS sings to the dead not with grief but with a kind of tender acceptance — as if death were simply the final room in a house we all must eventually enter. The tiger is not the enemy. It was always part of the journey.

Stop III Death as Liberation

Ca Dao Mẹ Mother's Folk Song

Sài Gòn, 1973

Mẹ ngồi ru con, tiếng hát ầu ơ
Tiếng hát ngàn năm còn mãi xanh tươi...

"Mother sits rocking her child, lullaby ầu ơ / A song of a thousand years, still forever green..."

By the early 1970s TCS had arrived at something like peace with death. In this late song, the dying is held inside a lullaby. Mother, child, earth, and song are one continuous cycle. We die back into the voice that first named us. Death is not ending — it is return.
